# Drawer

Drawer is used to overlay content on top of the interface. They are intended to capture the user's attention in order to inform or shift focus to a pertinent task.

## Usage

Drawers overlay content on top of the interface. They capture the user's attention and inform or shift focus to a pertinent task.

At UiPath, modals focus the user's attention exclusively on one task. Drawers are for adding new or informational content.

## Anatomy

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Header** | Title and close control for the drawer |
| **Slot component** | Area for embedded components |
| **Text content** | Body content of the drawer |
| **Footer** | Contains primary and secondary action buttons |

## Sizes

| Size | Width |
|---|---|
| **Large** | 600px |
| **Medium** | 440px |

## Layout

Drawers provide contextual information and references related to the experience or current page. They slide in from the right side of the screen and display an overlay curtain over the previous content.

- Drawers are always placed on the right side of the screen
- Primary and secondary buttons in the footer and header are always right-aligned
