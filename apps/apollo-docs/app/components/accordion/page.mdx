# Accordion

Accordions are a list of vertically stacked headers that expand and collapse to reveal hidden content blocks. They help reduce scrolling and shorten pages.

## Usage

Accordions are composed of a list of longer blocks of text, with a header button to toggle the visibility of each block. They are well-suited for compactly presenting grouped content with collapsible panels.

Use an accordion:

- To organize related information
- To group and hide content to keep an interface clean and reduce clutter
- When space is at a premium and long content can't be displayed all at once

## Anatomy

Accordions enable multiple content sections to be displayed in a limited space and collapsed or expanded by the user.

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Accordion header** | Label for representing a section of content that also serves as a control for showing the section |
| **Chevron icon** | Indicates if the panel is open or closed |
| **Accordion panel** | The section of content associated with an accordion header |
